    • 1. Sports Development

    From my exploration of the most successful countries in the Olympics it has become very clear that the GDP of a country has a huge impact on whether a country succeeds or not. Countries such as the USA, Russia and China dominate while smaller nations or less developed countries such as much of Africa have little or no medals at all.

    Recommendations:

    • Develop grassroots programs in underrepresented countries to broaden competition
    • Foster partnerships between established sports nations and developing ones to exchange expertise

    2. Gender Equality

    Gender inequality is extremely prevalent within the Olympic's history. It was only in 1900 that women were allowed to compete in the games and it was only in the London games 2012 that every country competing had a women on their team. However it is evident from my analysis that the olympics is becoming much more equal in terms of gender although true equality has not yet been reached. (In the 2024 games there were equal medals for both men and women however my chosen dataset does not surpass 2016)

    Recommendations:

    • Ensure equal medal events for men and women in all sports
    • Encourage participation from underrepresented genders by promoting campaigns and increasing funding.

    3. Introduction of new sports

    Throughout olympic history the sports included have grown and changed. This is done in order to keep the games fresh and exiting while also appealing to younger audiences. In the LA games 2028 Baseball, Softball, Cricket, Lacrosse, Squash and Flag football will all be added to the schedule.

    Recommendations:

    • Continuously review and adapt the Olympic program to reflect evolving global interests
    • Consider more regional sports to attract diverse participation

    4. Environmental Sustainability

    The Olympics is probably the biggest sporting event on the planet. It attracts millions of tourists and costs billions to run. It also has a huge impact on the environment releasing millions of tons of Carbon Dioxide into the atmosphere every 4 years. A recent change has occurred lately however as The International Olympic Committee has pledged to make the games as green as possible.

    Recommendations:

    • Avail of already existing infrastructure and stadiums instead of building everything from scratch
    • Ensure that any venues or infrastructure created for the games have a purpose after the games conclude
    • Use Venues that are closer to each-other or are accessible by public transport
    • Remove the use of single use plastics in venues

    5.Historical legacy and global collaboration

    The Olympics throughout history has always been an event that has shown the best of humanity. It is a place where every nation is equal and where political rivalries have been put aside. The Olympics have inspired younger generations throughout the years, promoting health, fitness and sport as well as fostering the next generation of athletes. The Games have also put on show many unique and diverse cultures throughout the world displaying the best best that humanity have to offer.

    Recommendations:

    • Use the games as a platform to promote peacebuilding
    • Highlight stories of resilience, inclusivity, and shared humanity through the Games
    • Use the games as a means to share cultures and traditions
